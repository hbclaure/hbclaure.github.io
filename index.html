Welcome!

My name is Houston Claureand I am a postdoc at Yale University in the Computer Science Department. My interest lie in the field of human-robot interaction(HRI), human-computer interaction(HCI), and artificial intelligence. In my research, I look to developing algorithmic and design solutions that allow robots allocate both physical resources(materials, tools, etc.) as well as social resources(attention, gaze) while considering human notions of fairness. Specifically, I am using reinforcement learning and machine learning techniques to develop algorithms to enhance human-robot teams. I am also interested in the design of robotic systems capable of thriving in spaces inhabited by humans. I am working with Dr. Marynel VÃ¡zquez in the Interactive Machines Group (IMG).

I obtained my Ph.D. from Cornell University in the Sibley School of Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering. I was advised by Dr. Malte Jung and worked in the Robots in Groups Lab. I was also the founder of Vida, a company that uses artificial intelligence to create solutions that support teachers struggling with remote teaching during the COVID-19 lockdown.
